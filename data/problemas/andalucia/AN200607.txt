Sean $k, p, n \in \mathbb{N}$, con $0 \leq k \leq p \leq n.$

<ol>
    <li>
        Demostrar:
        $$\binom{n}{k}\binom{n-k}{p-k} = \binom{p}{k}\binom{n}{p}.$$
    </li>

    <li>
        Demostrar:
        $$\binom{n}{0}\binom{n}{p} + \binom{n}{1}\binom{n-1}{p-1} + \binom{n}{2}\binom{n-2}{p-2} + \dots + \binom{n}{p}\binom{n-p}{0} = 2^p\binom{n}{p}.$$
    </li>
</ol>
